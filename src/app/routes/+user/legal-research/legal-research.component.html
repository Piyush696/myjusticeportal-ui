Legal Research Assistant

Overview and Instructions

The Demo County Jail contracts with an outside firm to provide legal research assistance to the inmates of this facility. The company has been providing legal research support to inmates since 1995 and currently serves over 150 correctional facilities around the country. As with a traditional law library, this service is for informational purposes only. No legal advice is provided. You can utilize the service to obtain legal information as you require and in support of your constitutional right of access to the courts.​ ​

The following are tips to assist you in submitting a request form:

  • You may request up to five items per form. If you require more than five items, you will need to submit an additional form.
  • You may only submit up to three (3) forms per calendar month.
  • Requests must be related to your current charges, an appeal, a civil rights matter, a conditions of confinement concern, or a family law matter.
  • Inmates found to have abused this program will be subject to suspension.
  • Not all fields are required, however: the more information you provide, the more likely the information you receive will be relevant and useful to you.
  • When indicating the information you require, ask for specific cases, citations or code sections whenever possible. 
  • If you're unsure of which case, citation or code to request, you may request general information about various legal topics. For example: Theft Crimes, Information about Parole Violation, Statute of Limitations, Divorce, Arraignment Procedures or Habeas Corpus.
  • When requesting a specific case, provide as much information as you can such as case name, citation and year of decision. For example: People v. Smith, 123 Ga.App. 456, (1997).
  • If you require a specific legal form or sample motion please indicate the court you intend to petition. For example: "Writ of Habeas Corpus for Georgia state court" or “Sample Motion for Discovery for Federal Court”
